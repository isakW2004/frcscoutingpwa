Our Robot

Our Robot, named The Machine, features a push resistant mecanum drivetrain, a climber, and a shooter that can shoot low goals. The climber can easily climb the first or second rung.
Mecanum drivetrains often have a reputation of being easily pushed around. That's why we developed Teenage Resistance, our enhanced drive straight system. The robot uses sensors to detect where it is on the field, then if it is pushed, it will actively return to its previous position.
Our robot also features an LED Matrix display, easily communicating the robot's state back to the driver. This includes which balls are in the intake, where they are, if the robot is in autonomous, and the battery status of the robot.

Our Team

FRC® Team 2530, Inconceivable, from Rochester, Minnesota builds kids who build robots. We encourage the pursuit of learning through science, technology, engineering, and mathematics; additionally we promote Gracious Professionalism, student leadership, and teamwork in all aspects of our students’ lives. We model cooperation as we are composed of about 45 students from three schools and mentors from a variety of professions. Team 2530 operates with strong communication which is crucial in the successful coordination and organization of year-round volunteering events and technical training. With an atmosphere of creativity and innovation, Team 2530 inspires future engineers through community involvement, spreading the message of FIRST®, and meaningful connections with sponsors and mentors. Team 2530 is comprised of students from three rivaling high schools and two middle schools, we exemplify Coopertition. FRC® is one of the few extracurricular activities that transcends school boundaries and creates a unified team. All school colors and rivalries are set aside for Inconceivable Lime Green.
